> "Acquiring wisdom is a moral duty as well as a practical one." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life/mental-models.

> "You're going to advance in life by what you're going to learn after you leave here." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life/mental-models.

> "I'm not entitled to have an opinion on this subject unless I can state the arguments against my position better than the people do who are supporting it." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: decision-making/mental-models.

> "If you want to persuade, appeal to interest not to reason." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: mental-models/decision-making.

> "Generally speaking, envy, resentment, revenge and self pity are disastrous modes of thought." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life/mental-models.

> "Self-pity is not going to improve the situation." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life.

> "The highest form which civilization can reach is a seamless web of deserved trust. Not much procedure, just totally reliable people correctly trusting one another." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life/business/ethics.

> "If your proposed marriage contract has 47 pages, my suggestion is do not enter." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life.

> "Inversion frequently will solve problems which nothing else will solve. > "Without lifetime learning you people are not going to do very well. You are not going to get very far in life based on what you already know." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life/mental-models.

> "The really big ideas carry 95% of the freight." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: mental-models.

> "If you're unreliable it doesn't matter what your virtues are, you're going to crater immediately." — [USC Law Commencement 2007, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/). Topic: character-life/business.

## Source Class C — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom (USC Business 1994)

> "If the facts don't hang together on a latticework of theory, you don't have them in a usable form." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: mental-models.

> "You've got to have multiple models—because if you just have one or two that you're using, the nature of human psychology is such that you'll torture reality so that it fits your models." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: mental-models.

> "All the wisdom of the world is not to be found in one little academic department." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: mental-models.

> "80 or 90 important models will carry about 90% of the freight in making you a worldly-wise person." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: mental-models.

> "If you don't get this elementary, but mildly unnatural, mathematics of elementary probability into your repertoire, then you go through a long life like a one-legged man in an ass-kicking contest." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: mental-models/decision-making.

> "You don't need many insights in a lifetime. If you look at Berkshire Hathaway and all of its accumulated billions, the top ten insights account for most of it." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: investing/decision-making.

> "When you get a few, you really load up. It's just that simple." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: investing/decision-making.

> "Over the long term, it's hard for a stock to earn a much better return than the business which underlies it earns." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: investing/business.

> "Advantages of scale are ungodly important in determining which businesses succeed and fail." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: business.

> "It's a terrible problem to get big bureaucracies to behave." — [USC Business 1994, via Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Topic: business.
